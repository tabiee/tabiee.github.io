Project Overview


Steam Page

Stellar Stables is a 2D casual point-and-click adventure about collecting alien horses. You navigate the world through interactive elements on the map, solve puzzles and play minigames to get all the horses on the alien planet you are stationed in.

This game was made by a team of six students, the development lasted 3.5 months and the game was released on Steam by the end.
I was involved primarily in Programming and Art, while also sharing the role of the Product Owner.

Programming

I was in charge of the main mechanics for the game, which involved clicking UI elements, interaction and inventory systems.
Additionally, I oversaw Steam intergration, helped with UI and made minor animations.

Biggest Take-aways:


Divided Tasks

Me and another programmer on our team divided our tasks so that we did not overlap and could work without relying on each other too much. It was effective to keep our workflow smooth. I found that to be a good approach.

Editing Assets

I went through three different inventory assets before finally settling on one that fit our project. The first was too complex for me to edit quickly and the second was much simpler but had terribly dirty code that was difficult to work with. Trying out assets took out about 2 weeks of development, but was worthwhile as a learning experience to not waste time with bad assets.

Instructions Are Great

Writing clear instructions in the Inspector for other people to understand was a great way to keep the project organized and easy to work with. It meant that I did not need to spend as much time explaining how the system works to our implementer and left valuable reminders for myself.

Flowcharts

While we did not end up using our flowchart much since mine and the other programmer's tasks were divided, it was a good way to visualize the game's structure. We could see where our systems overlapped and what gaps we need to fill. It was a good way to plan out the overall mechanics of the game.
