Overview


The game is a rogue-like bullethell about plant people visiting planets infested by bug-like aliens, killing them and stealing their scrap to sell it. It took inspiration from games like Binding of Isaac and Gungeon, giving the player weapons with unique bullet patterns and various abilities, such as dashing.

Nature's Bounty was a small project with a week dedicated to preproduction, learning Agile and Scrum, and one week dedicated on actual development. The game concluded with a bossfight, despite many features not being made and the artstyle remaining inconsistent due to poor planning with the artists.

What I learned

At it's core, the bullethell mechanics were interesting and had potential to provide multiple gameplay angles that we did not have time to implement. The game had a complete loop, allowing the player to finish a few levels and beat the boss. I had learned a lot about modularity, design patterns and the use of ScriptableObjects and classes to store data for customization.

What I learned:


  • Preproduction
  • Planning a short game
  • Using the Command pattern
  • Bullethell mechanics
  • Using ScriptableObject to store bullet data for unique and customizable bullet formations
  • Working another programmer
  • Objectpools
  • Prioritizing tasks on a tight schedule
  • Explored various other patterns, such as Factory, Observer and Strategy
  • Style agreement matters for a coherent game
  • Making backlogs
  • Agile
  • Health & Damage systems
  • Collectibles
  • Modularity
  • Input System
